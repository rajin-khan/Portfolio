# Uses mark sources

The Uses directory loads local transparent PNG logomarks from `marks/`. They
keep their natural silhouette and aspect ratio: there is no visible badge
shell, square tile, white field, outline, or rotation. The generic desk lamp and
self-hosted rows use custom white SVG marks because there is no useful brand to
represent. No runtime logo CDN is involved. Product names, characters, and
trademarks remain the property of their respective owners.

## Hardware and accessories

- `apple.png`: silver Apple silhouette from Simple Icons. Reused for both
  MacBooks and AirPods.
- `raspberry-pi.png`: Raspberry Pi mark from Simple Icons, preserving
  the Raspberry Pi brand red. Follow the
  [Raspberry Pi trademark rules](https://www.raspberrypi.com/trademark-rules/).
- `xiaomi.png`: Xiaomi mark from Simple Icons in official orange.
- `logitech.png`: current Logitech wordmark from Wikimedia Commons, whose
  source is Logitech's press media library.
  [Source](https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Logitech_logo.svg)
- `fenda.png`: F&D mark from Fenda's official F580X support page, with the
  original dark image field removed.
  [Source](https://en.fenda.com/index.php/Home/Article/detail/value/Support/id/537.html)
- `royal-kludge.png`: RK wordmark from Royal Kludge's official site
  logo. [Source](https://en.rkgaming.com/)
- `generic-lamp.svg`: custom minimal desk-lamp mark drawn for this page; there
  is no known lamp brand to represent.
- `kaloc.png`: KALOC's official site favicon. [Source](https://en.kaloc.cn/)
- `transcend.png`: Transcend's horizontal mark, checked against the company's
  [official branding guide](https://transcend.com.tw/tmrc/images/brandingGuideline.pdf).
- `hoco.png`: white HOCO wordmark from the company's official site.
  [Source](https://hocotech.com/)
- `miniso.png`: MINISO shopping-bag mark from the official brand site.
  [Source](https://www.miniso.com/brand/)

## Applications and self-hosting

`arc.png`, `cursor.png`, `raycast.png`, and `docker.png` use their transparent
Simple Icons logomarks in recognizable brand colors. `iterm2.png` isolates the
green prompt from the installed macOS app artwork, omitting its square app-icon
field. `chatgpt.png` similarly keeps only the white ChatGPT knot from the
[ChatGPT logo artwork](https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:ChatGPT_logo.svg).
Cursor is also documented on its [official brand page](https://cursor.com/brand).
`self-hosted.svg` is a custom home-and-server mark drawn for the self-hosted row.
`docker.png` remains available for a future Docker-specific entry but is not
used by the current Uses page.

## Subscription services

- `railway.png`: white Railway logomark from Simple Icons, chosen for contrast
  on the dark page.
- `youtube-premium.png`: YouTube's red play mark from Simple Icons, used for
  the YouTube Premium subscription. YouTube usage guidance is available from
  the [Google Brand Resource Center](https://about.google/intl/us_en/brand-resource-center/).
- `netflix.png`: Netflix N symbol from Simple Icons in Netflix Red (`#E50914`),
  following the color and container guidance on the
  [official Netflix brand site](https://brand.netflix.com/en/assets/logos/).
